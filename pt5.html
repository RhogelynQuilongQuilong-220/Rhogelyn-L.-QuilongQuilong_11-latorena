Bagnet

Bagnet is a famous Filipino dish from the Ilocos Region in Luzon, particularly associated with Narvacan and Vigan City, Ilocos Sur. It is made from pork belly that is boiled, air-dried, and deep-fried until the skin becomes golden brown and extra crispy while the meat remains tender and juicy. Known for its rich flavor and crunchy texture, bagnet is commonly served with steamed rice and a vinegar-based dipping sauce, and it is also used as an ingredient in traditional Ilocano dishes such as pinakbet. Ilocos Empanada is a famous Filipino street food from the Ilocos Region in Luzon, especially in Batac City, Ilocos Norte, and Vigan City, Ilocos Sur. It is made with a crispy orange rice flour pastry filled with grated green papaya, mung bean sprouts, egg, and Ilocos longganisa. The empanada is deep-fried until golden and crispy, then served with a spiced vinegar dipping sauce. It is well known for its crunchy texture, savory filling, and unique Ilocano flavor. Pinakbet is a traditional Filipino vegetable dish from the Ilocos Region in Luzon, particularly popular in Ilocos Norte, Ilocos Sur, La Union, and Pangasinan. It is made with a variety of vegetables such as bitter melon, eggplant, squash, okra, string beans, and tomatoes, cooked with fermented fish sauce (bagoong) and often mixed with pork or shrimp. Known for its rich, savory flavor and nutritious ingredients, pinakbet is one of the most famous dishes of the Ilocano people and is commonly served with steamed rice.

VISAYAS

                 

Cebu Lechon

Binagol

Piaya

Cebu Lechon is a world-famous Filipino roasted pork dish from Cebu Province in the Visayas, particularly in Cebu City, Cebu. It is made by roasting a whole pig over charcoal while it is stuffed with herbs and spices such as lemongrass, garlic, onions, and black pepper, giving it a rich, flavorful taste without the need for sauce. Known for its crispy golden skin and juicy, tender meat, Cebu Lechon is a favorite centerpiece at fiestas, celebrations, and special occasions throughout the Philippines. Binagol is a traditional Filipino delicacy from the Eastern Visayas, particularly in Dagami, Leyte. It is made from mashed taro (gabi) mixed with coconut milk, sugar, condensed milk, and egg yolks, then wrapped in coconut shells and steamed. Known for its rich, creamy texture and sweet flavor, Binagol is a popular delicacy and souvenir from Leyte that is often enjoyed as a dessert or snack. Piaya is a famous Filipino flatbread from the Western Visayas, particularly in Bacolod City, Negros Occidental. It is made from unleavened dough filled with muscovado sugar, then flattened and cooked on a griddle until golden brown. Known for its thin, crispy crust and sweet caramel-like filling, piaya is a popular snack and one of the most famous delicacies and pasalubong from Negros Occidental.

Sinuglaw is a popular Filipino appetizer and main dish that originated in Davao City, Mindanao, known for its unique combination of smoky grilled pork belly and fresh, tangy raw fish ceviche. Its name is a portmanteau of two distinct cooking methods common in Visayan and Mindanaoan cuisines: Sinugba (meaning "to grill" or "barbecue") and Kinilaw (meaning "to cook or cure in vinegar"). It is widely celebrated as the ultimate pulutan (beer match or finger food) and comfort food. Curacha is a deep-sea spanner crab (Ranina ranina) that is a highly sought-after seafood delicacy native to Zamboanga and the Sulu Archipelago in southwestern Mindanao. Its name translates to "cockroach" in the local Chavacano dialect because of its peculiar, segmented body shape.
