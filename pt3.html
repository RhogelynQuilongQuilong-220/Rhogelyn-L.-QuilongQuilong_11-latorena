DESCRIPTION

Mount Apo is a massive, dormant solfataric stratovolcano on the island of Mindanao and serves as the highest mountain peak in the Philippines. Towering at an elevation of 2,954 meters (9,692 feet) above sea level, it is known throughout the country as the "Grandfather of Philippine Mountains" and represents the ultimate bucket-list destination for local and international mountaineers.

DESCRIPTION

The Hinatuan Enchanted River is a deep, brackish spring river in Hinatuan, Surigao del Sur, Philippines. Famous for its crystal-clear sapphire and jade waters, it flows about 300 meters into the Philippine Sea. It features an unexplored underwater cave system and a mesmerizing daily fish-feeding ritual at noon

DESCRIPTION

Aliwagwag Falls, located in Cateel, Davao Oriental, is widely celebrated as the highest and widest cascading waterfall in the Philippines. Known for its stunning staircase-like boulders, it is often described as a "Stairway to Heaven" and is a centerpiece of the Aliwagwag Protected Landscape.

DESCRIPTION

Mount Apo is the highest mountain and volcano in the Philippines, towering at 2,954 meters (9,692 ft) above sea level. Located on the island of Mindanao, it is a flat-topped stratovolcano with sulfur vents and is revered as a sacred, ancestral site by indigenous tribes.

DESCRIPTION

Camiguin is a pear-shaped, volcanic island province in the Philippines' Bohol Sea, just off the northern coast of Mindanao. Known as the "Island Born of Fire," it features seven majestic volcanoes in a land area of only 292 square kilometers. It offers a unique mix of white sandbars, black-sand beaches, hot and cold springs, and towering waterfalls
